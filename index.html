I am Zoe, a researcher in
Human Computer Interaction
based in Chicago.



I am a graduating master student in Computer Science at the University of Chicago building wearables and haptic devices. My recent work with my colleagues, HandMorph: a Passive Exoskeleton that Miniaturizes Grasp, won the Best Paper Award from ACM UIST. Previously, I studied Electrical Engineering with a concentration in Control Systems at NC State University and conducted series of research in Mixed Reality and Eye Tracking.

I am keen to seek out new guiding principles and concepts that seamlessly integrate groundbreaking technologies and techniques with natural human dexterity.

My long-term research goal is to develop systems to promote co-adaption of computational tools and users to assist and augment human creativity and experiences.

I plan to achieve this goal by exploring new paradigms in HCI and developing novel interactive devices, enabling technologies and fabrication tools that bring independence and freedom in both the digital and physical world to diverse audiences.

I am learning how to unicycle while wondering what would be the next. What about you?
